Top 5 AI News of the Week

1

Women in AI and OpenAI Leadership Changes

TechCrunch has initiated a series of interviews to spotlight remarkable women in the field of AI, such as Sandra Watcher, a professor of data ethics at Oxford, and Sarah Kreps, a professor of government at Cornell. This initiative aims to acknowledge the contributions of women academics and professionals in AI, where they have historically been underrepresented.

In other news, OpenAI announced that Sam Altman would rejoin the company's board of directors as CEO. This move comes alongside other organizational changes to strengthen OpenAI's governance and strategic direction.

2

ChatGPT Memory and Google's Gemini Launch

OpenAI has introduced memory capabilities to ChatGPT, allowing it to remember details from previous interactions for more personalized and contextually relevant responses. This enhancement marks a step towards making AI conversational agents more valuable and nuanced in their interactions. Meanwhile, Google announced Gemini, an AI chatbot designed to replace its earlier Bard service. Gemini's launch reflects Google's ongoing efforts to compete in the rapidly evolving AI chatbot space, aiming to offer users a more capable and flexible digital assistant​.

3

Innovations in AI-Assisted Health and Robotics

Developments include a 'Smart Glove' designed to improve hand mobility for stroke patients, showcasing how AI and robotics are being leveraged to offer tangible benefits in healthcare. Another breakthrough is an artificial muscle device capable of producing force 34 times its weight. These innovations promise enhanced robotic functionalities and potential applications in prosthetics and assistive devices, underscoring the interdisciplinary impact of AI in health and engineering​.

4

MIT's Contributions to AI

MIT News covers a range of AI advancements, including peripheral vision enhancement in AI models, which could significantly improve driver safety and provide insights into human behavior. MIT spinout DataCebo is using generative AI to bolster datasets for better software testing, showing the practical applications of AI in improving software reliability. Another highlight is the work of Lightmatter, aiming to achieve light-speed computing using photonic technologies, which could revolutionize chip communication and calculations, pushing the boundaries of current computing technologies​.

5

Microsoft's AI Research and International AI Developments

A team of AI researchers at Microsoft developed the Orca-Math small language model, which has outperformed larger models in standardized math tests, demonstrating that efficiency in AI can sometimes be achieved with smaller, more focused models. Internationally, Italy and Brazil are taking regulatory steps to manage the impact of AI, with Italy investigating OpenAI's new video tool Sora and Brazil seeking to regulate AI-generated deepfakes ahead of elections.

CX Genie Weekly News & Updates

CX Genie’s Version 2.9 Release

This latest update to our platform focuses on streamlining the setup process and integrating more mobile-friendly features. These updates and aimed at ensuring that customer interactions are smoother, more intuitive, and highly efficient. 

1. Simplified Dashboard Setup

Setting up your CX Genie just got easier! With a floating button on the dashboard's bottom right corner, users can now easily access a comprehensive guide to configure the bot. This detailed walkthrough is designed to assist in completing the bot settings thoroughly and quickly, so you can jump right into improving your customer's experiences.

2. Enhanced Bot Functionality

Auto-reply settings have been made more flexible. You can now toggle the Auto-reply feature on or off for all conversations—past, present, and future—in Live Chat and Tickets with a single switch in the bot's Settings. For convenience, Auto Reply can also be adjusted for individual conversations, giving you more control over your automated responses.

3. Live Chat Improvements

  • Immediate Recognition of New Messages: Never miss a message again! Conversations with unread messages will now be highlighted with a bold dark green dot and displayed in bold, so you're always aware of new customer interactions.
  • Advanced Search Capabilities: Searching for specific content within conversations is now more straightforward. This enhancement allows for the easy retrieval of necessary information without the hassle of scrolling through entire chat histories.

4. Mobile App Integration: A Step Forward with Flutter

  • Full Message Flow Functionality: Leverage the complete capabilities of Message Flow when integrating CX Genie with your mobile app, enhancing customer interaction through your bot.
  • Default Message Flow: You can now set a default Message Flow for the bot, which will be displayed in a new conversation on your mobile app, streamlining engagement.
  • Video Rendering in Chat: CX Genie's mobile integration now supports seamless video playback within chat conversations, enriching the customer experience.

5. Addressing the Bugs

We've squashed several bugs to enhance your CX Genie experience, including synchronization issues with Facebook page messages, redirection errors from notifications, missing data after bot onboarding, live chat crashes related to Facebook conversations, and formatting issues in message flow quotes.
